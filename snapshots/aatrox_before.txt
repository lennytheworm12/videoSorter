
=== ability_windows ===
  - The W (Infernal Chains) pull window: When the target is dragged back to the center, they are briefly locked in a displacement animation; use this exact moment to guarantee the sweet-spot hit of the Q3 (The Darkin Blade) for maximum burst damage.
  - E (Umbral Dash) animation cancel: You can cast E during the wind-up of any Q cast to reposition the hitbox mid-swing; use this to catch enemies who attempt to sidestep the sweet-spot zones, effectively turning a missed Q into a hit.
  - W (Infernal Chains) vulnerability window: The ability has a long cooldown (20-12s). If you miss your W, you lose your primary setup for landing the full Q combo; play defensively for the next 10+ seconds as you lack the CC to prevent enemies from simply walking out of your Q sweet spots.
  - R (World Ender) fear window: The initial fear from R can be used to interrupt channeled abilities (like Teleport or high-value ultimates); time your R activation to overlap with an enemy's channel to force a cancellation and gain a massive tempo advantage.
  - P (Deathbringer Stance) trade window: Your passive auto-attack deals max health damage and heals you; always weave this auto-attack between Q casts rather than at the end of a combo to maximize your sustain during the trade and reset the cooldown faster through ability usage.
  - E (Umbral Dash) mobility window: Because E is your only dash, using it aggressively to close the gap leaves you vulnerable to ganks or counter-engage; against champions with high-impact skillshots (like Ahri E or Lux Q), hold your E specifically to dodge their CC rather than using it to initiate.
  - Q (The Darkin Blade) cooldown window: At early ranks, the 14s cooldown is your primary window of weakness. If you commit all three Q casts and fail to secure a kill or force a summoner spell, you are effectively a 'sitting duck' for the duration of the cooldown; retreat immediately if the enemy survives the full rotation.
